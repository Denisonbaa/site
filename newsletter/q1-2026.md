## BAA Capital Collective - The Future of Black Alumni Philanthropy

The BAA is preparing to launch the next generation of alumni giving: the **BAA Capital Collective**.

The Capital Collective will be the primary way Black alumni contribute to the BAA’s annual operating budget, which is currently approximately $12,000 per year. That budget supports the core work of the Association, including student programs, communications, alumni engagement, financial administration, governance, and other priorities approved by the Executive Board.

The idea is simple: instead of relying mainly on occasional fundraising appeals, alumni will be able to make recurring or one-time contributions into a shared pool of resources. Small and moderate gifts, combined across the membership, can provide the BAA with a more predictable and sustainable financial base.

The Collective may also support selected strategic priorities beyond the annual budget, including work related to the BAA Endowment, Black student life, organizational infrastructure, and other long-term initiatives.

The Capital Collective is **not active *yet***. Denison currently serves as the BAA’s fiscal sponsor. Before launching the Collective, the BAA must establish a new fiscal-sponsorship infrastructure that provides clearer financial separation, reliable reporting, and full BAA control over how funds are managed and used. Our current fiscal sponsorship arrangement with Denison has served us well for many years, but is not sufficient to the task before us now.  The BAA has made application to [the Social Good Fund ](https://www.socialgoodfund.org/fiscal-sponsorship/about-our-sponsorship/)for fiscal sponsorship and our application is pending. Once that structure is in place, members will receive more information about contribution options, financial reporting, funding priorities, and the official launch.

The goal is not simply to create another fundraising campaign. It is to build a dependable way for Black alumni to finance the BAA’s work, strengthen its independence, and turn collective support into lasting capacity.
